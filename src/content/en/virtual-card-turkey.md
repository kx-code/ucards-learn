## Why Turkey Residents Need Virtual Credit Cards

Turkey has experienced one of the most severe currency crises among major economies. The Turkish Lira lost over 80% of its value against the US Dollar between 2020 and 2025, with annual inflation peaking above 70% in 2024. This economic environment has driven millions of Turks to seek alternatives for preserving purchasing power and making international payments.

Turkish banks impose strict foreign currency controls and charge hefty commissions on international transactions — often 3-7% above the mid-market rate. Many international merchants decline Turkish-issued cards altogether, and recurring subscriptions in USD or EUR become increasingly expensive as the Lira depreciates.

Virtual credit cards funded by stablecoins like USDT offer Turkish residents a way to bypass these restrictions entirely. By converting Lira to USDT and funding a virtual card, you lock in a stable value and gain access to worldwide online payments without relying on Turkish banks.
