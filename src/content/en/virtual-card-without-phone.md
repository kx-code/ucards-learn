## Why You Need a Virtual Card Without a Phone Number

Phone numbers were once the standard method for internet identity verification, but in 2026, they have become a major obstacle to privacy and convenience:

- **Privacy risk** — Phone numbers can be linked to real identities, addresses, and social media accounts
- **SIM swapping** — Attackers can use social engineering to hijack your phone number
- **Spam** — Providing your phone number often leads to a flood of marketing messages and calls
- **International user barrier** — Many platforms only support phone numbers from specific countries
- **Frequent changes** — Changing numbers requires updating all linked accounts one by one
- **Signal limitations** — Poor cellular signal in some areas makes it impossible to receive verification codes

Virtual credit cards that don't require a phone number solve all of these problems.

## Real Risks of Phone Number Leaks

### SIM Swapping Attacks

Attackers use social engineering to convince carriers to transfer your phone number to a SIM card they control. Once successful, they can:

- Receive all your SMS verification codes
- Reset passwords for all linked accounts
- Access your bank, email, and social media

uCards doesn't use phone number verification, fundamentally eliminating the threat of SIM swapping.

### Phone Number Identity Linking

Through a phone number, someone can find:

- Real name
- ID number
- Home address
- Social media accounts
- Workplace

Not providing a phone number = cutting off this information chain.

### Spam and Harassment

After providing your phone number, it may be sold to third parties:

- Marketing calls
- Spam messages
- Phishing links
- Harassment calls

### How Web3Auth Works

1. User connects a crypto wallet (such as MetaMask)
2. The wallet signs the authentication request with a private key
3. Web3Auth verifies the signature validity
4. A decentralized identity credential is created
5. Subsequent logins are automatic, no repeated action needed

The entire process transmits no passwords, stores no phone numbers, and is fully decentralized.
