## Germany's Credit System Barrier: The Schufa Problem

Schufa (Schutzgemeinschaft fur allgemeine Kreditsicherung) is the backbone of Germany's financial system. Nearly every bank queries Schufa scores when reviewing credit card applications:

| Schufa Issue                 | Impact                                                                         |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| No record for new immigrants | Credit card applications denied despite stable income                          |
| Student status               | Most banks refuse to issue credit cards to students                            |
| Lowered score                | A single late payment can prevent approval for years                           |
| Inquiry record impact        | Each application leaves a hard inquiry; frequent applications lower your score |
| Privacy concerns             | Schufa collects extensive personal spending and credit data                    |

An estimated **8 million people** in Germany struggle to obtain traditional credit cards due to Schufa score issues. This includes millions of EU and non-EU expats working in Germany.
