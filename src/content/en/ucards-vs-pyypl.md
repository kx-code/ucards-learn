## FAQ

### Can uCards and Pyypl be used at the same time?

Yes, and we recommend it. Use uCards for international online spending and subscriptions (zero KYC, low fees), and use Pyypl for local fiat spending scenarios. Both platforms complement each other — choose based on your needs.

### How are Pyypl's currency conversion fees calculated?

Pyypl charges 2-4% currency conversion fees on cross-border transactions. For example, if your card is denominated in USD but you spend in a non-USD region, Pyypl will convert at their exchange rate, which is typically 2-4 percentage points worse than the market rate. uCards uses USDT top-ups, and USDT is pegged 1:1 to USD, so this issue doesn't exist.

### What is the minimum USDT top-up amount for uCards?

uCards' minimum top-up amount varies by card type, typically between $10-$50. The specific amount is clearly displayed when opening a card. We recommend starting with the minimum amount to test that the card works properly, then topping up as needed.

### Can I use uCards if I don't use cryptocurrency?

uCards focuses on cryptocurrency top-ups and currently doesn't support direct fiat deposits. If you don't hold USDT, you'll need to first purchase USDT on an exchange (like Binance or OKX), then top up uCards. If you completely want to avoid cryptocurrency, Pyypl's fiat channel may be more suitable. But as USDT becomes more widely adopted, acquiring it is increasingly convenient.

### Do Pyypl's cards have an expiration date?

Pyypl's virtual cards have expiration dates, typically shown in the card information. uCards' virtual cards also have expiration dates, and the platform will remind you before they expire. The advantage of virtual cards is that they can be quickly replaced upon expiration, unlike physical cards that need to be mailed.

### Which platform has better customer support?

uCards provides online support with fast response times. Since uCards doesn't require KYC, there are no verification-related support needs — issues are relatively simple (mainly top-up and payment related). Pyypl also has online support, but some users report longer wait times during peak hours.
