## How to Pay Netflix with Crypto: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Create your uCards virtual card

Visit [ucards.uk](https://ucards.uk) and sign up. No KYC, no documents, no waiting. Your virtual Visa card is created instantly.

### Step 2: Top up with cryptocurrency

Fund your card with your preferred crypto:

- **USDT (TRC-20)** — Recommended. $1 transfer fee, 3-minute confirmation
- **USDT (ERC-20)** — Ethereum network, slightly higher fees
- **ETH** — Direct Ethereum transfer
- **BNB (BEP-20)** — Ultra-low fees on BSC

For Netflix, add $10-30 depending on your plan. Include a $3-5 buffer for fees.

### Step 3: Add your card to Netflix

1. Log in to your Netflix account at [netflix.com](https://netflix.com)
2. Go to **Account** > **Manage payment info**
3. Select **"Credit or Debit Card"**
4. Enter your uCards card number, expiry date, and CVV
5. Set the billing address (use any valid US or local address)
6. Click **"Save"** to confirm

Netflix will make a small authorization charge to verify the card. This is normal and the amount is refunded.

### Step 4: Enjoy Netflix

Your subscription is now active and paid with crypto. Netflix will charge your card automatically each month.

If your card doesn't have enough balance, Netflix will:

2. Send you an email notification

Simply top up your card and Netflix will retry automatically.

## Netflix + Crypto: Common Questions

**Does Netflix accept crypto directly?**
No. Netflix only accepts credit/debit cards and PayPal. A virtual card converts your crypto into a Visa payment that Netflix accepts.

**Can I share my Netflix account paid with crypto?**
Yes. Your payment method doesn't affect Netflix's sharing policies. Standard plan allows 2 simultaneous streams; Premium allows 4.

**Will Netflix know I'm paying with crypto?**
No. Netflix sees a standard Visa card transaction. The crypto funding is invisible to Netflix.

**What if I want to change Netflix plans?**
Simply change your plan on Netflix's website. The new amount will be charged to your uCards card on the next billing date. Make sure your balance covers the new price.
