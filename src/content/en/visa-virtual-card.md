## What Is a Visa Virtual Card?

A Visa virtual card is a digital payment card that operates on the Visa network. It provides you with a 16-digit card number, expiration date, and CVV — the same details you would find on a physical Visa card. The difference is that these details exist only in digital form.

You use a Visa virtual card exactly the same way you would use a physical Visa card for online purchases: enter the card number, expiration date, and CVV at checkout.

### Key Characteristics

- **Network:** Visa (the largest card network globally)
- **Form factor:** Digital only — card details delivered electronically
- **Funding:** Prepaid (loaded before spending) or credit-linked
- **Acceptance:** Same as any physical Visa card for online transactions

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is a Visa virtual card the same as a Visa prepaid card?

A Visa virtual card is a type of Visa prepaid card that exists only digitally. All virtual cards are prepaid (you load funds before spending), but not all prepaid cards are virtual — some come as physical plastic. The payment functionality is identical.

### Can I add a Visa virtual card to Apple Pay or Google Pay?

It depends on the provider. Some Visa virtual card issuers support adding the card to mobile wallets like Apple Pay and Google Pay. Check with your specific provider for mobile wallet compatibility.

### Will my Visa virtual card work internationally?

Yes. Visa virtual cards work at any online merchant worldwide that accepts Visa. There are no geographic restrictions on online transactions, though some providers may have country-specific policies.

### How is a Visa virtual card different from a Visa gift card?

A Visa virtual card is reloadable — you can add funds multiple times. A Visa gift card is pre-loaded with a fixed amount and typically cannot be reloaded. Virtual cards also usually have more features like the ability to freeze the card, view transactions, and manage settings through an app or dashboard.

### What happens if my Visa virtual card is declined?

First, check that your card has sufficient balance for the purchase including any currency conversion fees. If the balance is adequate, the merchant may not accept prepaid cards (rare but possible for certain high-risk transactions). Try a different merchant or contact your card provider for assistance.
