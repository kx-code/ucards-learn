## The Online Payment Challenge in Egypt

Egypt's digital economy is expanding fast. With over 75 million internet users and an e-commerce market growing at 30% annually, more Egyptians are shopping and paying for services online than ever before. Yet a major obstacle stands in the way: most Egyptian debit cards do not work for international payments.

Egyptian banks issue Visa and Mastercard debit cards, but these cards are typically restricted to domestic transactions. Even "international" cards from banks like CIB, Commercial International Bank, and Arab African International Bank frequently fail when used on global platforms. The Central Bank of Egypt imposes strict controls on foreign currency spending, and banks often block international transactions by default, requiring customers to visit branches and request activation.

For the millions of Egyptians who want to subscribe to Netflix, buy from Amazon, pay for ChatGPT Plus, or run Facebook Ads for their business, these restrictions are a daily frustration. Currency devaluation adds another layer of complexity — the Egyptian pound lost over 50% of its value against the USD between 2022 and 2025, making prepaid USD cards expensive and scarce.

Virtual credit cards funded by cryptocurrency bypass all of these barriers entirely.
