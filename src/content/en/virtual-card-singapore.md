## Singapore Regulation FAQ

### Is cryptocurrency legal in Singapore?

Yes, completely legal. Singapore is one of the most crypto-friendly countries in the world. The Payment Services Act (PSA) was enacted in 2019, and cryptocurrency exchanges operate legally under the MAS licensing framework. The Digital Payment Token (DPT) service licensing regime was also introduced in 2022.

### How are cryptocurrency taxes handled in Singapore?

Singapore does not have a capital gains tax, so profits from buying and selling cryptocurrency are tax-free for individuals. This is a significant advantage. However, if cryptocurrency trading is conducted as a business, it may be subject to income tax.

### Does MAS regulation affect uCards usage?

uCards is an overseas service outside the scope of MAS regulation. Individuals topping up USDT and using a virtual card for online payments does not conflict with MAS regulations.
