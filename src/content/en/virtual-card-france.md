## Why French Residents Need a Virtual Credit Card

France has a well-developed banking system, but for many residents — especially new immigrants, students, freelancers, and privacy-conscious users — obtaining a credit card for international online payments is no easy feat. French bank account opening procedures are notoriously bureaucratic, typically requiring proof of identity (Carte d'identite or Passport), proof of address (Justificatif de domicile — a utility bill in your own name), proof of income, and sometimes an employment contract from your employer.

More critically, almost all French financial products require an **IBAN** (International Bank Account Number). For foreigners who have just arrived in France, without a fixed address you cannot open a bank account, without a bank account you have no IBAN, and without an IBAN you cannot get a credit card — a classic chicken-and-egg problem.

France's **Carte Bleue** (the French version of a Visa debit card) is very convenient for everyday spending, but it carries foreign transaction fees (2-3%) and some merchant compatibility issues for international online payments, USD-denominated subscriptions, and cross-border shopping.
