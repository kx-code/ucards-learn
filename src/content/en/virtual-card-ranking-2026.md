## FAQ

### What's the difference between a virtual credit card and a physical credit card?

A virtual credit card has no physical plastic card — it only has a card number, expiry date, and CVV, specifically designed for online payments. The advantages are extremely fast issuance (uCards takes just 2-5 minutes), lower fees, and no risk of loss or theft. Some platforms like RedotPay and Dupay also offer physical card options.

### Are cryptocurrency-funded virtual cards legal?

Yes, entirely legal. Crypto virtual cards are essentially prepaid cards — you top up with your own crypto assets, and the platform converts them to fiat balance for spending. Visa cards issued by compliant platforms like uCards are accepted by legitimate merchants worldwide.

### How secure is uCards?

uCards employs multiple security measures: no collection of personal identity information (zero data leak risk), funds held via blockchain custody, card freezing available at any time, and TLS 1.3 encrypted communication. Compared to platforms that require ID and selfie uploads, uCards offers a higher level of privacy security.

### Can virtual cards be linked to Apple Pay or Google Pay?

Some platforms support this. uCards' mobile wallet compatibility depends on the specific card type. For online spending scenarios, you can simply enter the card number to complete payment — linking to a mobile payment wallet isn't always necessary.

### Can funds topped up to a virtual card be refunded?

Most platforms support withdrawing card balance. uCards allows you to withdraw your card balance back to your original wallet address, though a small network fee may apply. We recommend topping up only what you need to avoid keeping large amounts on the card long-term.
