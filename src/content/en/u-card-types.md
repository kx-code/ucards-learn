## 2. By Format: Virtual Card vs. Physical Card

### 2.1 Virtual Card

A virtual card exists purely in digital form — no plastic. You receive a 16-digit card number, expiration date, and CVV immediately after registration, usable only for online payments.

**Advantages:**

- Instant access — no waiting for mail delivery (under 5 minutes)
- Lower issuance fee ($0 – $2.50)
- Stronger privacy — no physical card to lose
- Fund only the exact amount you need to limit your risk exposure

**Limitations:**

- Cannot be used for in-person payments
- Cannot be swiped or inserted at a POS terminal

### 2.2 Physical Card

Some U Card platforms also offer physical cards — plastic cards with printed card numbers that work at POS terminals.

**Advantages:**

- Supports in-store payments and ATM withdrawals (on select cards)
- Works both online and offline

**Limitations:**

- Must wait for mail delivery (1–4 weeks)
- Higher issuance fee ($10 – $50+)
- If lost, you need a replacement

| ----------------- | --------------------- | ------------------ |

| Online Payments   | Yes                   | Yes                |
| In-Store Payments | No                    | Yes                |

## 8. Common Misconceptions

### Misconception 1: "A better card network means a better card"

Not true. The differences between Visa, Mastercard, and Discover are minimal for online payments. What matters is your use case — if you only use ChatGPT Plus, a $1.00 Visa AI Card is a better fit than a $2.50 Mastercard Universal Card.

### Misconception 2: "Having multiple cards is expensive"

The uCards Visa Ads Card is free, and the Visa AI Card costs only $1.00. A multi-card strategy costs very little but gives you better risk isolation and budget management.

### Misconception 3: "A Universal Card works for everything"

Universal Cards have broad coverage, but **purpose-built cards have higher success rates in their specific niches**. For example, the Ads Card is optimized for Facebook/Google risk-control systems and achieves noticeably higher approval rates than a Universal Card when funding ad accounts.

### Misconception 4: "Discover cards don't work well"

Discover has strong acceptance in North America — Amazon, eBay, and other major e-commerce platforms all support it. Some smaller overseas merchants may not, but for most online spending scenarios, Discover works perfectly fine.
