## What Is USDT?

USDT (Tether USD) is a cryptocurrency stablecoin pegged 1:1 to the US dollar. One USDT always equals one US dollar — it does not experience the dramatic price swings of BTC or ETH. This makes USDT the closest thing to a "digital dollar" in the cryptocurrency world.

### USDT at a Glance

| Metric                | Figure                                |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Market capitalization | Over $140 billion                     |
| Daily trading volume  | Over $50 billion                      |
| Supported blockchains | TRON, Ethereum, BSC, Solana, and more |
| Price peg             | 1 USDT = 1 USD                        |
| Issuer                | Tether Limited                        |

### Why USDT Is the Most Popular Stablecoin

- **Price stability** — Pegged 1:1 to the dollar, eliminating crypto volatility concerns
- **Wide circulation** — Supported by virtually every cryptocurrency exchange
- **Low fees** — Especially on the TRON network, where each transaction costs less than $1
- **Fast confirmation** — On TRON, transactions confirm in 1–3 minutes

## What Is a USDT Virtual Card?

A USDT virtual card is a virtual credit card that accepts USDT top-ups. It converts your USDT into a USD balance spendable on the Visa network, letting you use USDT at any online merchant worldwide that accepts Visa.

Put simply, a USDT virtual card is a bridge — turning your USDT into a credit card that can buy products on Amazon, pay for a Netflix subscription, or top up a ChatGPT Plus account.

### How a USDT Virtual Card Works

```
Your USDT → Top up on card platform → Converted to USD balance → Spend via Visa card number
```

The entire process takes just a few minutes:

1. You send USDT to the top-up address provided by the platform
2. The blockchain network confirms the transaction (1–15 minutes, depending on the network)
3. The platform converts USDT to a USD card balance
4. You can spend using the card number immediately

**Important**: Always select the correct network when sending USDT. If the platform gives you a TRC20 address (starting with T), you must send from the TRON network. Cross-chain transfers will result in permanent loss of funds.

After selecting top-up on the uCards platform, you will see a deposit address and amount. Open your crypto wallet or exchange:

Once the top-up arrives, your USD card balance updates immediately. Use the Visa card number to make purchases at any online merchant.
