## What Is a U Card?

A **U card** is a colloquial term for a virtual credit card funded by USDT (Tether) or other cryptocurrencies. Since USDT is commonly called "U" in the Chinese crypto community, virtual cards funded by USDT became known as "U cards."

### How U Cards Work

```
Crypto Wallet → USDT Top-up → U Card Platform Conversion → Visa/Mastercard Balance → Online Spending
```

1. You hold USDT in a cryptocurrency exchange or wallet
2. Transfer USDT to the U card platform via TRON/Ethereum/BSC network
3. The platform converts USDT to USD balance at 1:1 ratio
4. You receive a Visa/Mastercard virtual card
5. Spend at any online merchant that accepts Visa/Mastercard

## FAQ

### What's the difference between a U card and a regular credit card?

A U card is a prepaid virtual card — you need to top up before spending, and there's no overdraft or credit limit. The U cards issued by uCards are Visa cards that look no different from regular Visa credit cards to merchants, so the spending experience is identical. The difference is: U cards are funded with USDT, require no bank account, no credit check, and no KYC (with uCards).

### Are U card funds safe?

uCards employs a dual security mechanism of blockchain custody plus platform risk control. Your USDT is converted to USD balance after top-up and stored in compliant custodial accounts protected by the Visa card network.
