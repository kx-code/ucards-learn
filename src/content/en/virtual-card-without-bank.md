## Why Is It So Hard to Get a Credit Card Without a Bank Account?

In the traditional financial system, credit cards and bank accounts are deeply intertwined. Banks rely on your account history, income records, and credit score to decide whether to issue you a card. Without a bank account, the doors of traditional finance are almost entirely closed.

### How Many People Worldwide Lack Bank Accounts?

According to World Bank 2025 data, approximately **1.3 billion adults globally have no bank account**. The reasons include:

| Reason                | Share | Description                                                      |
| --------------------- | ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Lack of documentation | 25%   | Cannot provide the identity documents banks require              |
| Fees too high         | 20%   | Bank accounts have monthly fees and minimum balance requirements |
| Too far away          | 15%   | Rural areas lack bank branches                                   |
| Distrust of banks     | 15%   | Lack of confidence in financial institutions                     |
| Religious reasons     | 10%   | Islamic law prohibits interest                                   |
| No need for a bank    | 10%   | Cash or e-wallets are sufficient                                 |
| Other reasons         | 5%    | Poor credit history, age restrictions, etc.                      |

### The Barriers of Traditional Credit Cards

| Barrier              | Description                                                                | How Many Are Shut Out           |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Bank account         | You must have one to apply                                                 | 1.3 billion people              |
| Government ID        | Requires a valid government-issued ID                                      | Hundreds of millions            |
| Credit history       | Requires an existing credit track record                                   | Young people, new immigrants    |
| Proof of income      | Requires a stable income source                                            | Freelancers, students           |
| Proof of address     | Requires utility bills at a fixed address                                  | Digital nomads, migrant workers |
| Country restrictions | Residents of certain countries can hardly get an international credit card | Multiple countries              |

**The result**: An estimated 2–3 billion people worldwide cannot access what would traditionally be considered a credit card.
