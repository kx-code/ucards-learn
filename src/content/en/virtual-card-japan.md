## Credit Cards in Japan: Why Getting One Is So Difficult

Japan's credit card screening process is among the strictest in the world. Card approval is a major obstacle for the following groups:

| Barrier                       | Affected Groups                           | Specific Issue                                                                         |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Strict credit checks**      | Foreign residents, international students | Residence status, income verification, and years of employment are closely scrutinized |
| **Credit history dependency** | Newcomers, first-year workers             | No registration with Japan's credit bureaus (CIC/JICC) leads to automatic rejection    |
| **Long processing times**     | Those who need a card urgently            | Approval takes 2-4 weeks, plus another week for card delivery                          |
| **Annual fee burden**         | Students, part-time workers               | Even standard cards carry annual fees of 1,000-5,000 yen; gold cards exceed 10,000 yen |
| **Overseas transaction fees** | Users of international services           | Foreign currency transaction fees of 2-3%, plus unfavorable exchange rate markups      |
| **Low credit limits**         | Young adults, freelancers                 | Monthly spending limits are often capped at 100,000-300,000 yen                        |

### Unique Challenges for Foreign Residents

Approximately 3.3 million foreign nationals reside in Japan (as of 2025), and many face disadvantages during credit screening. If the remaining validity of your residence period is less than one year, rejection is almost guaranteed. Even those with permanent residency or Japanese citizenship may be affected if there are indications of plans to leave the country. This situation poses serious challenges for IT engineers, international students, English teachers, and many others working in Japan.

uCards is an overseas service outside the scope of FSA regulation. Personal use by Japan residents does not violate FSA regulations. However, profits from cryptocurrency trading must be reported as "miscellaneous income" on your Japanese tax return.
