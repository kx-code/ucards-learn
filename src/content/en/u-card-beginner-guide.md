## Step 3: Fund Your Card with USDT

This is the key step. After selecting a card type, you pay the issuance fee and fund the card using USDT.

### 3.1 Set Your Funding Amount

1. On the card type page, click "Open Card"
2. Enter the amount you want to load onto the card (minimum $10)
3. The system automatically calculates the total you need to pay

### Fee Calculation Example

Say you choose a Visa Universal Card and fund it with $50:

| Item              | Amount     |
| ----------------- | ---------- |
| Issuance Fee      | $2.00      |
| Funding Amount    | $50.00     |
| Funding Fee (2%)  | $1.00      |
| **Total You Pay** | **$53.00** |
| **Card Balance**  | **$50.00** |

> **Note**: Your card balance equals your funding amount. The issuance fee and funding fee are additional costs — they do not count toward your card balance.

### 3.2 Choose a Payment Network

The system lets you choose which blockchain network to use for sending USDT. Here is how the three options compare:

| Network  | Protocol | Fee    | Arrival Time | Recommendation     |
| -------- | -------- | ------ | ------------ | ------------------ |
| TRON     | TRC20    | < $1   | 1–3 minutes  | Highly recommended |
| BSC      | BEP20    | < $0.5 | 1–5 minutes  | Recommended        |
| Ethereum | ERC20    | $2–$20 | 3–15 minutes | Optional           |

**For beginners, TRON (TRC20) is strongly recommended.** Reasons:

- Lowest fees (under $1)
- Fastest arrival
- The most widely used network for USDT

### 3.3 Send USDT

The system will display a unique deposit address and the exact payment amount. Follow these steps:

#### Detailed Instructions

**Step 1: Copy the deposit address**

On the uCards page, you will see:

- An address starting with `T` (TRC20 format)
- The exact amount you need to send

Click the copy button next to the address to copy it to your clipboard.

**Step 2: Open TronLink**

Click the TronLink icon in the top-right corner of your browser to open the wallet.

**Step 3: Initiate the transfer**

1. Click "Send"
2. Paste the address you just copied into the recipient field
3. Enter the exact amount shown by the system (**must be precise to two decimal places**)
4. Confirm that the network is set to TRC20

**Step 4: Confirm and send**

Double-check the following:

- Is the recipient address correct?
- Does the amount match what the system displayed (including the decimal suffix)?
- Is the network set to TRC20?

Once everything checks out, click "Confirm Send."

### Important Payment Notes (Read Before Sending)

- **Amount must be exact**: uCards confirms your payment by matching the exact deposit amount. If the amount is off, the payment may not be confirmed automatically.
- **Network must match**: If the system shows a TRC20 address (starting with `T`), you must send via TRC20. Sending on the wrong network can result in permanent loss of funds.
- **Do not send directly from an exchange**: Some exchanges restrict transfers to personal addresses or delay withdrawal processing. We recommend first withdrawing to your personal wallet, then sending from your wallet to uCards.
- **Keep your transaction record**: If anything goes wrong, the transaction hash (TxID) is the key to tracking and resolving the issue.

## Troubleshooting Common Beginner Issues

### Issue 1: Payment Sent but Status Stuck on "Waiting for Payment"

**Possible causes and solutions:**

| Cause                     | Solution                                                  |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Wrong network selected    | Verify that you sent to the correct network address       |
| Amount mismatch           | Confirm the sent amount matches what the system displayed |
| Blockchain congestion     | Wait longer (especially on ERC20)                         |
| Transaction not broadcast | Check whether your wallet shows the transaction as "Sent" |

If the payment is still unconfirmed after 30 minutes, note the transaction hash and contact uCards support.

### Issue 2: Card Payment Declined

| Cause                 | Solution                                                 |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Insufficient balance  | Make sure your card balance exceeds the purchase amount  |
| Over limit            | Check if you exceeded the daily or per-transaction limit |
| Merchant risk control | Try a small test transaction ($1–$2) first               |
| Card type mismatch    | Confirm the merchant supports your card network          |

### Issue 3: Sent USDT on the Wrong Network

If you sent ERC20 USDT to a TRC20 address (or vice versa), the funds may be permanently lost. This is one of the most serious errors in blockchain transactions.

**Prevention:**

- Double-check the network type before sending
- TRC20 addresses start with `T`
- ERC20 addresses start with `0x`
- Do a small test transaction ($1–$2) first to confirm the process works

### Issue 4: Miscalculated Funding Amount

Remember this formula:

```
Amount to Send = Funding Amount + Issuance Fee + Funding Fee (2%)
Card Balance = Funding Amount
```

Example: Funding $100 on a Visa Universal Card:

- Funding amount: $100
- Issuance fee: $2.00 (first-time only)
- Funding fee: $100 x 2% = $2.00
- Amount to send: $104.00
- Card balance: $100.00

## Best Practices for Beginners

### Fund Management

- **Fund small amounts frequently**: Only load what you need for the near term — avoid keeping large idle balances
- **Use TRC20 first**: Always fund with TRC20 for the lowest fees
- **Leave a buffer**: Add 5–10% extra when funding to prevent running just short

### Security Tips

- **Back up your wallet seed phrase**: Write it on paper and store it somewhere safe
- **Never share card details**: Your card number, expiration date, and CVV are sensitive
- **Review transactions regularly**: Check spending history under "My Cards"
- **Avoid public Wi-Fi for payments**: Use a private network for financial transactions

### Spending Tips

- **Start with a small transaction**: When using a new merchant for the first time, test with a $1–$2 purchase
- **Keep enough balance for subscriptions**: Recurring charges will auto-deduct — make sure your balance can cover them
- **Understand refund policies**: Some card types do not support refunds — check before spending
- **Use the right card for the job**: AI subscriptions on an AI Card, ads on an Ads Card — it is more efficient
